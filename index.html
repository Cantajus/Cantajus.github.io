Who was the absolute genius that made this all possible?

Glen Bell was an American entrepreneur who founded Taco Bell, one of the most popular fast-food chains in the world today. Born in 1923 in Lynwood, California, Bell’s journey to creating Taco Bell began in the 1950s, when he opened his first restaurant, Bell's Drive-In, which served hot dogs and hamburgers. However, his real breakthrough came after he experimented with Mexican-style food. After observing a nearby Mexican restaurant’s success, Bell decided to introduce tacos to the Southern California market. He modified traditional recipes, simplifying the ingredients to suit American tastes, and opened the first Taco Bell in Downey, California, in 1962. Bell’s vision for Taco Bell was to make Mexican food fast, affordable, and accessible to a wide audience, which contributed to the chain’s rapid growth. By the 1970s, Taco Bell had expanded across the U.S., and by the 1980s, it became a major player in the fast-food industry. Bell sold Taco Bell to PepsiCo in 1978, which further fueled the chain’s growth. Glen Bell’s innovative approach to fast food and his ability to adapt traditional Mexican dishes into convenient, mass-market offerings helped shape Taco Bell into the global brand it is today. His legacy continues through Taco Bell's enduring popularity and its widespread influence on the fast-food industry.
